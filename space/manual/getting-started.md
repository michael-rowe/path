# Getting started

Your first ten minutes with Path.

## What Path is for

Path keeps a record of your **professional development** — the things you do that develop your practice — and links those activities to the **standards** of whatever framework you're working towards (promotion criteria, professional body standards, fellowship descriptors).

It does this by separating the work into a small set of record types:

| Kind of record | What it is | When to use it |
|---|---|---|
| CPD entry | A specific activity — a course, conference, project, teaching | Whenever you do something professionally significant |
| Reflection | A structured account of what you took from an experience | When the activity invites genuine learning |
| Claim | An argued statement that you meet a particular standard | When you have several CPD entries that together make a case |
| Evidence | A separate page for a tangible artefact (PDF, certificate, feedback letter) | Only when an activity record alone doesn't capture the artefact |
| Task | A to-do item attached to an active Path | When you notice work to do later, on a specific Path |
| Quick capture | A passing thought to come back to | When you notice something but cannot stop to process it |
| Path | A goal you're working towards (a promotion, fellowship, registration cycle) | When starting a new goal |

> **tip** You don't need to make all of these for every activity. Most days you'll only make one CPD entry. Reflections and claims accumulate over months.

## Your first action

Click the **+ Capture** button in the Navigator (or press `Ctrl-Alt-c` from anywhere). A small menu will appear asking what you want to make.

For your first time:

1. Pick **Quick capture**.
2. Type a name — anything descriptive, with hyphens between words. For example: `2026-05-03-noticed-attendance-pattern`.
3. The page that opens has prompts. Type a few sentences in each section. Save.

You've just made your first record.

## Where things live

Your records are organised into folders by kind:

- `cpd/` — your CPD entries (one per activity)
- `reflections/` — structured reflections
- `claims/` — your arguments that you meet a standard
- `evidence/` — tangible artefacts and the metadata pages that describe them
- `captures/` — quick thoughts not yet turned into anything else
- `paths/` — the goals you're working towards
- `criteria/` — the individual standards within each goal's framework

> **note** You don't need to put files in folders manually. When you create a record using the Capture menu, it goes to the right place automatically.
