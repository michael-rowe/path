# What to record, and what kind of record to make

The hardest question in any portfolio system is *"is this worth recording, and if so, how?"* This page gives you an answer.

## The Capture menu

Click **Capture something** on the home page (or press `Ctrl-Alt-c`). You will see six options:

| Option | Use it when |
|---|---|
| **CPD activity** | You did something — a course, conference, piece of teaching, project meeting, supervision session — and want to log it |
| **Reflection** | You want to think structurally about an experience, separately from the activity record |
| **Claim** | You are ready to argue, in writing, that you meet a particular standard |
| **Future-claim** | You are writing about your aspirations and forward-looking plans |
| **Quick capture** | A thought has landed; you want to keep it without committing to anything bigger |
| **Path** | You are setting up a new goal (promotion, fellowship, registration) |

## Decision guide

> **tip** Most days, you only make a **CPD activity** entry. Everything else is for less frequent moments.

```
Did I do a specific activity (course, conference, teaching, project)?
  └─ Yes → CPD activity

Did something happen that I want to think structurally about?
  └─ Yes → Reflection (separate from the CPD entry, linked to it)

Am I ready to write the argument that I meet a particular standard?
  └─ Yes → Claim

Have I had a thought I want to come back to, but cannot process now?
  └─ Yes → Quick capture
```

## What goes in a CPD entry

A CPD entry is the **atomic unit of evidence**. It records *what happened*, not what you took from it. Reflection is a separate record.

The template asks for:

- **Context**: what was the activity? Where, with whom, why this one?
- **What I did**: your concrete role and contribution. First person. Avoid vague language like *"was involved with"*.
- **What changed**: outcomes. Numbers and dates where you have them, qualitative description where you do not. Distinguish your contribution from collective work.
- **Reflection**: the immediate sense of what mattered. *Brief.* If the experience invites a deeper structured reflection, make a separate Reflection record and link to it.

> **warning** Resist the urge to write everything in the CPD entry. The discipline of separating *what happened* from *what I made of it* makes both stronger.

## What goes in a Quick capture

A passing thought. A pattern you noticed. A name to follow up. A draft idea. Two sentences is enough.

Quick captures appear on the home page as **"Quick thoughts to come back to"** until you turn them into something else (a CPD entry, a link in a claim) or change their `status` field from `unprocessed` to `processed`.

> **note** The capture is not the work. It is a marker for future you, saying *"this mattered, look again"*.

## When to write a Claim

When you can answer **all three** of these:

1. *Which standard or criterion am I claiming?* (You can name the code: 1.1, 2.3, etc.)
2. *Which CPD entries together support this claim?* (You can list at least three.)
3. *What does this body of work demonstrate that no single piece does on its own?* (You can answer in one paragraph.)

If you cannot answer all three, you are not ready to write the claim. Keep building CPD entries.
