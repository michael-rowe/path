# D4.3 — Establishing effective organisational policies and/or strategies for supporting and promoting others

> Descriptor 4: Principal Fellow

This is the criterion most under-evidenced in failed D4 applications. Authoring or shaping a policy is the *artefact*; the *evidence* is what happened next. Did the policy change subsequent practice? Was the strategy adopted, and by whom, and with what effect? Without that follow-through, this criterion remains thin.

## Indicators

- Authorship of, or significant contribution to, institutional or sector policies on teaching and learning
- Leadership of strategy development with measurable adoption (number of programmes affected, staff reached, decisions influenced)
- Influence on policy through committee chairing, advisory roles, or commissioned contributions
- Evidence the policy or strategy has demonstrably shaped subsequent practice — not just been published
- Acting as a change agent, opinion leader, mentor, or leader of a community of practice
