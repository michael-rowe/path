# D4.4 — Championing and influencing the development of others

> Descriptor 4: Principal Fellow

Influence beyond your home institution. Sector leadership, professional body roles, invited international keynotes, and mentoring others to fellowship status all count — but the evidence is whether *they* succeeded, not whether you *attempted to lead*. The strongest claims here are those where the impact on the development of others is itself measurable.

## Indicators

- Sustained leadership in professional bodies, learned societies, or sector organisations
- Leadership of national or international communities of practice
- Invited keynotes, plenaries, and advisory roles attesting to standing in the field
- External recognition through awards, fellowships, editorial roles, or commissioned external reviews
- Mentoring of others to fellowship status (SFHEA, PFHEA) with evidence of their successful applications
- Demonstrable influence on practice beyond your home institution
