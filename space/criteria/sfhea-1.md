# D3.1 — Co-ordination, support, supervision, management, and/or mentoring of others

> Descriptor 3: Senior Fellow

D3.1 is about what you do *for and with* others in teaching contexts. The criterion is satisfied not by demonstrating your own excellent teaching, but by showing that you have co-ordinated, developed, or mentored colleagues in ways that extend and improve teaching practice more broadly. Breadth matters: one mentoring relationship is too narrow; a pattern across multiple colleagues, cohorts, or contexts is the right scale.

## Indicators

- Co-ordination or management of programmes, modules, or teaching teams
- Formal supervision or mentoring of colleagues in teaching practice (early-career staff, PGT students on teaching, etc.)
- Leadership of structured CPD or peer development activities for colleagues
- Leadership of communities of practice, learning sets, or peer review schemes
- Evidence that co-ordination or mentoring produced change — not just activity
- Breadth across multiple colleagues, cohorts, or contexts rather than one-to-one only
