# D3.3 — Substantive influence on student learning and/or the student learning environment

> Descriptor 3: Senior Fellow

D3.3 is the outcome criterion — it closes the causal chain opened by D3.1 and D3.2. It is not enough to have co-ordinated colleagues (D3.1) or engaged in developmental activities (D3.2); the application must demonstrate that these activities produced **substantive influence on student learning** or the environment in which learning takes place. The link between your activities and the student-facing outcomes must be explicit, not assumed.

## Indicators

- Evidence of improved student outcomes attributable to D3.1/D3.2 activities
- Changes to curriculum, assessment design, or student support resulting from your influence
- Student feedback or outcome data (retention, satisfaction, attainment) showing impact
- Institutional or programme-level changes to the learning environment that you led or enabled
- Evidence the influence is sustained and extends beyond a single module or cohort
- D3.3 is the outcome criterion — D3.1 and D3.2 are the activities; the link must be explicit in the application narrative
