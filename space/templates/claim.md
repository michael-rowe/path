## The claim

An argued statement, in first person, written for an assessor. Two to four paragraphs. State the claim, situate it (scale, longevity, scope), and signal the strongest piece of evidence.

## Evidence summary

Brief synthesis of the evidence chain. What does the body of work *collectively* demonstrate that no single piece does on its own? This is the interpretive layer — the part assessors need most.

## Quantified impact

The single most reliable way to lift a claim from credible to compelling is a number with a comparator. Aim for one or two lines per measure. *"Improved engagement"* is weak; *"Module engagement rose from 47% to 71% across two semesters (n=180), measured by weekly attendance and asynchronous submission rates"* is strong. If a measure is qualitative, name the source and the audience.
