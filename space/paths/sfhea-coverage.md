## Narrative — what good looks like for each criterion

The auto list above is structural. A few interpretive notes:

- **D3.1** should demonstrate *breadth* — evidence of co-ordinating or mentoring multiple colleagues in different contexts is more compelling than a single mentoring relationship. Co-ordination of a programme or team is the right scale. Show that the activity changed how others teach.
- **D3.2** should demonstrate *reach* — scholarship, external engagement, peer review, or resource creation that extends your influence beyond your direct team. External recognition (invited speaker, external examiner, editorial roles) strengthens this criterion considerably.
- **D3.3 is the outcome criterion** — D3.1 and D3.2 are activities; D3.3 is what they produced. The most common reason D3 applications struggle is that D3.3 evidence is asserted ("my mentoring improved student outcomes") rather than demonstrated with data, feedback, or documented curriculum change. Close the causal chain explicitly.
