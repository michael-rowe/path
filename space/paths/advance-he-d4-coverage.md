## Narrative — what good looks like for each criterion

The auto list above is structural. D4 needs evidence of *strategic leadership with measurable impact*, not just activity. A few interpretive notes:

- **D4.1** is best evidenced through **policy work**, **mentoring**, and **public advocacy** that demonstrate the values are *consistently adopted* across the practice you influence — not just present in your own. A claim here that only describes your own teaching is too narrow.
- **D4.2** wants **outcomes**, not roles. "I led X" is weak; "I led X, which produced Y measurable change in Z timeframe" is strong. Quantify wherever possible.
- **D4.3** is the criterion most under-evidenced in failed D4 applications. It requires that policy or strategy you have shaped has *demonstrably influenced subsequent practice*. The follow-through is the evidence, not the artefact.
- **D4.4** asks for influence **beyond your home institution**. Sector leadership, professional body roles, invited international keynotes, and mentoring others to fellowship status all count.
